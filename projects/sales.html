Sales Performance Analysis

Analysed B2B sales data across 19 countries and 7 product lines using MySQL. Normalised a flat dataset into a relational schema, then wrote queries using aggregations, JOINs, and window functions to surface revenue trends, top customers, and product performance over time.

Relational Schema

The raw data arrived as a single flat CSV. I normalised it into 4 relational tables to enable proper JOINs and demonstrate database design principles.

customers

  • customer_id (PK)
  • customer_name
  • contact_first / last
  • city, country
  • territory

products

  • product_code (PK)
  • product_line
  • msrp

orders

  • order_number (PK)
  • customer_id (FK)
  • order_date
  • status
  • year, month, quarter

order_items

  • id (PK)
  • order_number (FK)
  • product_code (FK)
  • quantity_ordered
  • price_each, sales
  • deal_size

Sample Query — Month-over-Month Growth

WITH monthly AS (
    SELECT
        o.year, o.month,
        SUM(oi.sales) AS monthly_revenue
    FROM order_items oi
    JOIN orders o ON oi.order_number = o.order_number
    GROUP BY o.year, o.month
)
SELECT
    year, month,
    ROUND(monthly_revenue, 2)                          AS monthly_revenue,
    ROUND(SUM(monthly_revenue)
        OVER (PARTITION BY year ORDER BY month), 2)    AS ytd_revenue,
    ROUND(monthly_revenue
        - LAG(monthly_revenue) OVER (ORDER BY year, month), 2) AS mom_change,
    ROUND(
        (monthly_revenue - LAG(monthly_revenue) OVER (ORDER BY year, month))
        / LAG(monthly_revenue) OVER (ORDER BY year, month) * 100
    , 2)                                               AS mom_growth_pct
FROM monthly
ORDER BY year, month;
